&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp	3.2 million more people filed for unemployment benefits last week, reaching a total of 33 million. The month of April experienced a 14.7% unemployment rate, with 51.3% of the population unemployed, and 78.3% of those unemployed are temporary layoffs. The health care sector lost 1.4 million jobs. Historically, April generated the most tax revenue; however, this year the federal government has an April budget deficit of $737 billion, which is more than twice the amount of any other month in the past 40 years. California is suffering from a $54.3 billion detriment to the state budget, with future predictions of an 18% unemployment rate and a 25.5% decline in personal income tax revenue in the next fiscal year.
<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp	J. Crew filed for bankruptcy on May 4, becoming the first major retailer to fall victim to the economic consequences of the coronavirus pandemic. Disney’s movie studios are receiving 40% of the revenue earned last year. Factories from all around the globe are experiencing declines. The U.S. nonfarm sector lost more than 20 million jobs in April, with service businesses experiencing the most drastic fall in activity. Furthermore, house prices are increasing as both demand and supply in the house market decrease. In China, exports increased by 3.5% while imports decreased by 14.2%.
