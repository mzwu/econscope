COVID-19 Safety Information

What:

Covid-19 is an infectious respiratory disease. The coronavirus is spread from person to person, caused primarily through respiratory droplets from coughing and sneezing. Symptoms range from mild to severe, with most patients developing a fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. Some people with the infection are asymptomatic.

Do:

  • Wash your hands for 20 seconds with soap and water. If soap and water are unavailable, use a hand sanitizer containing at least 60% alcohol.
  • Cover your nose and mouth with a disposable tissue or flexed elbow when you cough or sneeze.
  • Avoid close contact (within 6 feet) with people who are sick.
  • Stay home and self-isolate from others in the household, especially if you feel sick.
  • Wear a face mask if you are sick, as recommended by the CDC.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces daily using common EPA-registered household disinfectants.

Don't:

  • Touch your eyes, nose, or mouth if your hands are not clean.
  • Leave your house unless seeking medical attention, going to work, obtaining groceries, etc.
  • Share personal household items such as dishes, drinking glasses, cups, eating utensils, towels, or bedding.
  • Congregate in gatherings of more than 10 people.

Sources:

  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
  • Virginia Department of Health (VDH)
  • World Health Organization (WHO)
